SAT & AP Help for Future Engineers

Get the scores, skills, and confidence you need to thrive in STEM.

Which APs Should You Take for Engineering?

  • AP Calculus AB/BC – foundational for all engineering majors
  • AP Physics 1 or C – essential for mechanical, aerospace, electrical
  • AP Chemistry – key for chemical, materials, and biomedical
  • AP Computer Science A or Principles – great for software & computer engineering
  • AP Statistics – useful in industrial, civil, or data-related fields

SAT Math Tips for Engineering Students

  • Master algebra, linear systems, trig, and functions
  • Practice both calculator & no-calculator sections
  • Get comfortable with time limits
  • Focus more on the math section (highest weight for engineers)

Suggested Study Timeline

10th Grade:

  • Start light SAT prep (30–60 min/week)
  • Explore SAT format and test structure
  • Begin planning future APs using your school's course catalog
  • Preview AP content over summer: math, physics, or coding

Summer Before 11th Grade:

  • Start full SAT prep plan
  • Review Course Exam Descriptions (CEDs) for planned APs
  • Take a diagnostic SAT test and analyze results

11th Grade:

  • Take the SAT at least once (spring or fall)
  • Start building strong study habits for spring AP exams
  • Use winter break for focused SAT or AP review

12th Grade:

  • Retake SAT if needed (early fall)
  • Finalize AP preparation
  • Focus on applications, recommendations, and scholarships

Final Notes

Don’t take every AP — take the ones that align with your strengths and goals. Colleges care more about quality, consistency, and challenge than quantity.

Colleges love to see students challenge themselves in a smart way, not just overloading.
