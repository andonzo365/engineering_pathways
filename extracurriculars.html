Extracurriculars for Future Engineers

Get involved, build skills, and explore your passions — outside the classroom.

1. Passion Projects

Create something you're genuinely curious about. It doesn’t need to be expensive or perfect — just creative and meaningful.

  • Solar-based battery
  • Recycled water purification system
  • Build a study planner app
  • Repair an old fan or engine

2. Internships & Job Shadowing

Explore the real world of engineering through job shadowing and internships.

  • Social media is a great platform to learn about opportunities!
  • Reach out to professors or engineers in your area
  • Email with a short intro and your resume
  • Even short visits or volunteering matter!

3. Clubs

Join or lead clubs that align with engineering skills.

  • Robotics Club, Science Fair, Math Team
  • FIRST Robotics, Girls Who Code, NSBE Jr.
  • STEM competitions, Hackathons, Olympiads

4. Online Courses & Certifications

Use online platforms to expand your skills outside school.

  • Coursera
  • edX
  • MIT OpenCourseWare
  • Harvard CS50

5. Building a Strong Resume and Application

  • Keep a document of your clubs, roles, and projects. Make sure to have proof!
  • Focus on what you have built, learned, or led
  • Link projects via Google Drive, GitHub, Notion, or blogs

6. Research Programs

Highly Competitive:

  • MIT RSI
  • Stanford RISE
  • Clark Scholars Program
  • Garcia Program, Simons Program

Other options:

  • Email local college professors or labs
  • Ask a science teacher to help you connect
  • Join community science programs or research fairs
  • Search online for virtual research options
  • Research on your own and publish your findings online

7. Passion Project Inspiration Sites

  • Instructables
  • Hackster.io
  • Science Buddies
  • Make Magazine
  • ArduCam Blog
  • Polygence – 10 Virtual Engineering Research Project Ideas
  • NovaScholar – 14 Mechanical Engineering Projects
  • Immerse Education – 100 Engineering Research Ideas

9. Document Your Journey

Keep track of your efforts and growth — great for recommendations and applications

  • Google Docs or Notion logs
  • Personal blog or project site
  • GitHub for code or tech projects

Final Advice

Finding a problem and working towards a solution is a great starting point. Remember don't try to do everything!
