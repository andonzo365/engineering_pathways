Extracurriculars for Future Engineers

How to explore engineering beyond the classroom in a meaningful, balanced way.

Why Extracurriculars Matter

Extracurriculars help colleges understand how you explore engineering outside of classes. The goal is not to do everything, but to go deeper into a few interests that genuinely excite you.

1. Passion Projects

Passion projects show curiosity, independence, and creativity. They do not need to be expensive, perfect, or award-winning to be meaningful.

  • Arduino-based weather or sensor projects
  • Recycled water filtration or sustainability projects
  • Simple apps or tools that solve everyday problems
  • Reverse-engineering household objects or mechanisms

2. Internships & Job Shadowing

Exposure to real-world engineering can come from short experiences. Even a few days of shadowing can help you understand what engineers actually do.

  • Email local engineers, labs, or startups with a short introduction
  • Ask teachers or family connections for introductions
  • Volunteer or assist with technical tasks when possible

3. Clubs & Competitions

Clubs help you collaborate, problem-solve, and build technical skills over time. Leadership and consistency matter more than the number of clubs.

  • Robotics Club, Science Olympiad, Math Team
  • FIRST Robotics, Hackathons, Engineering Olympiads
  • Girls Who Code, NSBE Jr., STEM-focused organizations

4. Online Courses & Skill-Building

Online courses can supplement school offerings and help you explore engineering fields not available at your school.

  • Coursera
  • edX
  • MIT OpenCourseWare
  • Harvard CS50

5. Research Opportunities

Research does not have to be formal or competitive to be valuable. Many students begin by working with local mentors or independent projects.

Highly Competitive Programs:

  • MIT RSI
  • Stanford RISE
  • Clark Scholars Program
  • Simons Research Program

More Accessible Options:

  • Email local college professors or research labs
  • Ask science teachers for mentorship connections
  • Participate in community science fairs or virtual research programs

6. Project Inspiration & Research Ideas

If you are unsure where to start, these resources provide structured ideas and inspiration.

  • Instructables
  • Hackster.io
  • Science Buddies
  • Polygence Engineering Research Ideas
  • NovaScholar Project Ideas
  • Immerse Education Research Ideas

7. Document Your Journey

Keeping track of your work makes applications, resumes, and essays much easier. Documentation also helps you reflect on growth.

  • Maintain a project or activity log in Google Docs or Notion
  • Create a simple portfolio or GitHub for technical work
  • Save reflections, photos, and outcomes from each experience

Final Advice

Focus on depth, curiosity, and consistency. Colleges value students who explore engineering thoughtfully rather than trying to do everything at once.
