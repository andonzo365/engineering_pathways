Explore by Grade: Your Engineering Roadmap

Find recommended classes, goals, and tips for each year of high school.

10th Grade: Build Your Foundation

Recommended Courses:

  • Geometry or Algebra II
  • Chemistry (foundation for chemical, materials, and bioengineering)
  • Intro to Programming or Computer Applications
  • General Science (if Physics is not offered yet)

Goals for the Year:

  • Strengthen your math & science fundamentals
  • Explore different fields through clubs and online courses
  • Join a STEM club (robotics, coding, science fair, etc.)
  • Try a small engineering passion project

Track Suggestions:

  • Thinking of Environmental, Bio, or Chemical Engineering? Focus on science and lab-based skills.
  • Interested in Software or Computer Engineering? Try Python or Java basics this year.

11th Grade: Explore and Specialize

Recommended Courses:

  • Pre-Calculus
  • Physics 1 or AP Physics 1
  • AP Computer Science Principles (or any logic/programming course)
  • Engineering Design, Drafting, or CAD (if offered)

Goals for the Year:

  • Explore one or two branches of engineering more deeply
  • Start building your resume: projects, competitions, research
  • Look for summer programs or internships
  • Try short “shadowing” experiences

Track Suggestions:

  • Mechanical or Aerospace Engineering? Focus on physics and build things like rockets or machines.
  • Environmental or Civil Engineering? Join a science fair or sustainability project.

12th Grade: Finalize and Apply

Recommended Courses:

  • AP Calculus AB or BC
  • AP Physics C: Mechanics or Electricity & Magnetism
  • AP Chemistry (especially for Chemical/Biomedical Engineering)
  • Capstone Project, Internship, or Shadowing

Goals for the Year:

  • Finalize college list (reach, match, safety)
  • Complete a strong personal or team project
  • Prepare engineering-focused college applications
  • Apply for scholarships early

Track Suggestions:

  • Chemical or Materials Engineering? Chemistry and lab experience help you stand out.
  • Computer or Electrical Engineering? Build something: an app, website, or circuit project.

General Note: These recommendations are general and apply to most types of engineering. If your school offers specialized electives (like architecture, electronics, biotech, or environmental science), take them! They can help tailor your application and skillset to your future field.

Reminder: Always check your school’s course catalog or speak with a guidance counselor to confirm which classes are offered and which ones fulfill graduation or prerequisite requirements. Every school is different, and planning early will help you make the most of your high school years.
